About the Canadian Collection of Agricultural Soil Microbes

The Canadian Collection of Agricultural Soil Microbes (CCASM) is a collection of bacteria isolated from sites across Canada. Most isolates come either from the soils of agricultural fields or crop research stations, although some come from other sites such as community or backyard gardens, or soils containing wild relatives of crop species. CCASM is hosted by Queen's University and the University of Manitoba, and was designed to facilitate the sharing of agricultural soil microbes across Canada. Answers to several common questions are provided below.

What is the purpose of CCASM?

CCASM is a bacterial culture collection developed for the purpose of storing bacteria isolated from agricultural fields across Canada. Many researchers have invested significant effort into isolating soil bacteria; however, these collections are generally only maintained in the researcher’s lab collection and are often not easily accessible by others. CCASM was designed to facilitate the sharing of bacterial samples across Canada, reducing duplication of efforts, and facilitating the discovery of microbes with agricultural or other biotechnological purposes by allowing researchers to screen already collected microbes for phenotypes of interest.

What micro-organisms can be deposited into CCASM?

CCASM primarily stores bacteria (archaea are also welcomed) collected from Canadian agricultural soils. Eukaryotic microbes, including arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi, are outside the scope of CCASM. CCASM primarily accepts soil microbes, including those isolated via direct plating of soil dilutions or through nodule trapping; microbes isolated from other crop tissues may also be considered for deposit following discussion with the CCASM curators. Bacteria (or archaea) isolated from non-agricultural sites are generally not appropriate for CCASM although in rare cases such microbes may be accepted following discussion with the CCASM curators. If unsure if your microbes are appropriate for CCASM, please reach contact the CCASM curators via email ccasm.collection@gmail.com .

Are all micro-organisms in CCASM publicly available?

All strains listed on the CCASM website are publicly available for delivery to researchers located in Canada.

Are there limits on the acceptable use of micro-organisms ordered from CCASM?

All microbes available through CCASM are allowed to be freely used for research purposes. Any commercial use of the material is excluded without prior written consent of Queen’s University or the University of Manitoba. Commercial use of products that are not regulated by the Nagoya Protocol is when the products are used directly for the purpose of profit generation or for a patent application. Internal use for quality control and as reference material is not a commercial use, unless the tests are performed for product approval and listed accordingly in reports for approval. For orders of microorganisms, commercial use is possible on a case-by-case basis with the consent of CCASM management.

How do I deposit a micro-organism into CCASM?

To deposit a strain, first navigate to the download Deposit page. Once there, complete and submit the form to initiate the process.

How do I browse the CCASM catalog?

CCASM allows for users to easily access the catalog of samples, and the associated metadata, by navigating to the search Browse page. Here, you can search for specific isolates or browse the full collection. A login is not required (nor possible).

How do I order a micro-organism from CCASM?

To order a strain, you need to first find the strain in the catalog via the search Browse page. Once you click on the strain of interest, a page with metadata and an “Add to Cart” button will pop-up. Click on the “Add to Cart” button to add the strain to your cart. Once you have selected all strains of interest, navigate to the shopping_cart cart page, and then complete and submit the form to initiate the process.

Who can order a micro-organism from CCASM?

Currently, CCASM strains can only be delivered researchers residing and working in Canada, and the strains can only be shipped to Canadian addresses. International orders are currently not possible.

Are there fees associated with ordering or depositing strains in CCASM?

Yes. While funding was secured to establish CCASM, we do not have ongoing funding to support its maintenance. Therefore, a small fee of $100/strain is charged for each strain being deposited into CCASM and for strain being delivered to a customer. Shipping fees are extra. Bulk discounts may be available for large orders but are not guaranteed. These fees help to ensure the continued viability of the collection.

I am a member of an ICT-CSAFS team. Do I get a discount?

Yes! During the duration of the ICT-CSAFS grants, deposits and access to CCASM are free-of-charge for other ICT-CSAFS teams, if the deposit/request relates to the ICT-CSAFS-funded research.

Does CCASM offer genome sequencing as a service?

Although we have in-house genome sequencing capabilities, CCASM is not a sequencing centre and so does not routinely offer genome sequencing as a service. However, CCASM curators are happy to discuss the possibility of sequencing bacterial (or archaeal) isolates that are being deposited to CCAM on a case-by-case basis, either for a fee or in exchange for authorship. Please contact the CCASM curators at email ccasm.collection@gmail.com if this is something that you would like to discuss.
