What is the purpose of CCASM?

The ultimate goal of the Canadian Collection of Agricultural Soil Microbes (CCASM) is to store microbe sample information collected by researchers around Canada. Researchers have already foregone collecting data, and will continue to do so in the future, but there is no centralized solution for sharing, storing or managing this data. CCASM is meant to fill that gap by creating a web portal where researchers can browse the catalog of samples as well as request physical samples of the microbes.

How do I browse the CCASM catalog?

CCASM allows for users to easily access the catalog of samples by navigating to the Browse section of the website. Here, you can search for specific samples. You do not need to log in to download strain data, just simply press download and a csv will be generated.

How do I request physical samples of a CCASM microbe?

If you find a sample you're interested in, you can request physical samples by clicking on the sample's details page and selecting the "Add to Cart" button. Once happy with selection, navigate to the cart page, where you will fill out a form to start the requesting process.

How do I deposit a strain to be added to CCASM?

If you have any strains that can be added to the collection, navigate to the Deposit page. There you will be prompted to fill out an excel document detailing the required information.
*The outcome of this deposit is determined by the admin team.
